Our Rich History

From humble beginnings to national impact - the journey of NSBE and our chapter through the decades.

1975

The Foundation

In April 1975, NSBE was founded at Purdue University by Edward Barnette and Fred Cooper as the Black Society of Engineers (BSE) to address the high dropout rate of Black engineering students. This marked the beginning of a movement that would transform engineering education across America.

First Chapter Founded
1976

National Formation

The first national meeting was held at the University of Illinois, officially establishing the National Society of Black Engineers. The iconic NSBE symbol with lightning bolts was chosen to represent members' burning desire for success and excellence in engineering.

National Organization
1981

Incorporation & Growth

NSBE was incorporated as a non-profit organization in Texas, with John Cason as the first elected president and Virginia Booth becoming the first female National Chairperson. This formalization enabled rapid expansion across universities nationwide.

Rapid Expansion
2002

UTSA Chapter Founded

The UTSA NSBE chapter was established, bringing the mission of academic excellence and professional development to the University of Texas at San Antonio engineering community. Our chapter began with a small group of dedicated students committed to making a difference.

UTSA Chapter Born
2025

Modern Era & Innovation

Today, NSBE boasts over 31,000 members across 99 pre-college, 250 student, and 68 alumni chapters worldwide. Our UTSA chapter continues to grow and innovate, leading initiatives in technology, sustainability, and community engagement while maintaining our core mission of excellence.
